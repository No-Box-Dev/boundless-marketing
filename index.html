Chapter One

The walls around our plans

We had a dream that many families recognise but few dare to act on. We wanted to live — really live — in different parts of the world.

But every time we imagined a new place, the same walls enclosed our plans.

What if they fall behind? What if we damage their future?
Chapter Two

We searched

Thoroughly, and for a long time. What we found disappointed us — not because the platforms were poorly made, but because they misunderstood what learning actually is.

Most were static and linear. Many were not built for families moving across different national curricula.

Chapter Three

If you cannot find it, build it

My background is in psychology. I understand how people learn, remember, and stay motivated.

For a while, I tried teaching our children directly. But when our oldest reached Year 8, keeping pace became almost a full-time job. For one child.

Chapter Four

Our children became curious again

Not the performed curiosity of a classroom. Real curiosity — the kind that wakes up early and wants to know why the fishermen leave before dawn.

Academically strong. Often ahead. And the world became the lesson.

Chapter Five

The platform that builds itself

No Box Education is not finished. It never will be. Our children use it every day — and when something could be better, they tell us.

Our daughter was eight when she programmed her first game for the platform. Then a second. Then a third.

And so we always ended up in places that were compromises — never our first choice. Education had quietly become the invisible fence around our lives.

What if high-quality education could evolve around our children, wherever we go?

A striking number had forgotten the deeper goal: to make children genuinely curious. To build the kind of intrinsic motivation that sustains a learner for life.

We found nothing that came close.
If you cannot find what you are looking for, build it yourself.

We gathered everything — learning science, developmental psychology, international curricula — and built, tested, and rebuilt until we were all satisfied.

The result is No Box Education. Where structure meets flexibility.

Where curiosity is treated not as a nice extra but as the entire point.
She did it because she had learned to believe: if you see something missing, you fill it.

Not just teach children what is already known — but grow children who build what does not yet exist.

The world is too big to only see it during school holidays.

How to get started

Three steps to a personalised learning journey designed around your child.

1

Complete your intake survey

Tell us about your child — their age, grade, strengths, and what they find challenging. This helps us understand where they are and where they want to go.

2

Personal chat with your student coach

After intake, you'll have a one-on-one conversation to align on learning goals, expectations, and your child's unique needs. No chatbots — a real person.

3

Your child starts their journey

We set up a personalised learning program. Your child logs in, starts practising, and the adaptive engine takes it from there — adjusting difficulty in real time.

Child dashboard

Adaptive exercises

939 exercises built on proven methods — Montessori, Singapore Math, bar models. Real pedagogy, not random worksheets.

Parent dashboard

Full visibility into what your child practised, where they excel, and what needs attention. No guessing — real data, per subject, per child.

Tutors see all assigned students, exactly where each child left off, and which exercises still need completing. Coordinate with parents seamlessly.
